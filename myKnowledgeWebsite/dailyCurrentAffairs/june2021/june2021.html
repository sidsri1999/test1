18 June 2021


Appointment: Aashish Chandorkar
Government appoints Aashish Chandorkar, as a counselor at the level of directors for India's Permanent Mission for 3 Years, at the World Trade Organization(WTO) in Geneva, Switzerland. He is a Private person, and an appointment of this kind is first at this mission. He is the Director of Bengaluru-based policy think tank Smahi Foundation of Policy and Research.


New Initiative: Rajasthan Government
In June 2021, Rajasthan government announced to set up Vedit Education and Sanskar Board to revive the knowledge of Ancient Sanskrit scriptures and connect the learnings of Vedas with Science and Yoga. It was promised by the government in its 2018 election Manifesto. The board will likely to set up in 4 to 5 months.


Loan: $484 million by ADB to India
In June 2021, Asian Development Bank(ADB) has given a loan of 484 million dollars to improve transport connectivity and facilitate Industrial development in the Chennai-Kanyakumari Industrial Corridor(CKIC) in the state of Tamil Nadu.


Line of Credit: 100 Million USD to Srilanka
In July 2021, India has signed an agreement of 100 million USD Line of Credit (LOC) to Srilanka for projects in Solar Energy Sector. This will ensure the Country's 70% energy consumption will be from renewable energy resources. by 2030.


New Campaign: Madhya Pradesh
In July 2021, Madhya Pradesh launched "Yuva Shakti Corona Mukti Abhiyan" (mean free from corona with youth power) to make people aware of the Covid Pandemic.


External: Bangladesh
Bangladesh will set up an international institute for the production of vaccines including COVID19. The government will sign an agreement with South Korea for this.


Sports: Rafael Nadal
Rafael Nadal announced that he will not play in Wimbledon and Tokyo Olympics.


Report: Global Peace Index 2021
India has ranked 135 in Global Peace Index 2021 by the Institute of Economics and Peace(IEP) based in Sydney, Australia. It is the 15th edition of GPI ranks 163 countries. Iceland remains at the top since 2008, followed by New Zealand, Denmark, Portugal, and Slovenia. From bottom countries are Afghanistan(4th time), Yemen, Syria, South Sudan, and Iraq.


Report: World Competitiveness Index
India maintained 43rd rank on the annual world competitiveness index compiled by the institute of management Development(IMD). It is calculated in 64 countries the top 5 countries are Switzerland, Sweden, Denmark, Netherlands, Singapore. Taiwan got8th rank reached in top 10 for the first time since the index was started 33 years ago.


Recognition: Sumant Sinha
United Nation's Global Compact has recognized Sumant Sinha, Chairman and MD of Renew Power, as among 10 SDG pioneers in 2021 for his work to advance access to clean and affordable energy.


Goal: Government
The government has set the target to reduce Road accident Deaths by 50% by the year 2024. DEAL: IOA and MPLInternational Olympics Association announced rupees 8 crore sponsor deal with MPL sports foundation till the end of 2022 and covering major events in the period

19 June 2021


New Initiative: Jahan Vote, Waha Vaccination
Delhi has launched the "Jahan Vote, Waha Vaccination" campaign, for mass vaccination at booth level in a planned manner.


New Appointment: V Vaidyanathan
IDFC First Bank has approved the proposal for the re-appointment of V Vaidyanathan as the MD and CEO for further 3 years.


New Scheme: Chief Minister Anuprati Coaching Scheme
Rajasthan has launched Chief Minister Anuprati Coaching Scheme to help underprivileged students prepare for entrance examinations to civil services and professional courses.


New Programme: Customized crash course program for COVID19 frontline workers
PM Narendra Modi launched a "Customized crash course program for COVID19 frontline workers". This will include 111 training centers and spread over 26 states. About 1 lac workers will be trained. There are six courses designed by top workers.


Agreement: India and Bhutan
India and Bhutan signed an agreement for developing cooperation between two countries in the area of the environment.


Exercise: IN-EUNAVFOR
Indian Naval Ship Trikand is participating in the maiden IN-EUNAVFOR(Indian European Union Naval Force) joint naval exercise which is going to happen in the Gulf of Aden. This exercise will include India with three EU nations(Spain, France, and Italy).In this five warships are participating from four Navies.


Obituary: Milkha Singh
India's sprint Icon Milkha Singh has passed away at the age of 91. He was also known as "The Flying Sikh". He was the only athlete to won gold in 400 meters race at the Asian games as well as the Commonwealth games. He also won the gold medal in the 1958 and 1962 Asian games. He was also awarded Padam Shri. He was remembered for his race in the 1960 Olympic games where he got 4th position in the 400-meter race.


Obituary: Kenneth Kaunda
Zambia's first president Kenneth Kaunda has passed away at age of 97.


Appointment: Justice Mahmud Jamal
Canada's PM Justin Trudeau has nominated Indian-origin Justice Mahmud Jamal to the Su preme Court of Canada. He is the first person of this color to be named to the apex court of the country.


Retirement: Kevin O'Brien
Ireland legend Cricketer Kevin O'Brien has announced retirement from One-Day International cricket.


New Launch: IndusEasyCredit
The IndusInd bank has launched a new platform "IndusEasyCredit" for both existing and non-IndusInd customers to avail of personal loans or credit cards on a single platform.


New Launch: "Shagun" Gift Card
LIC Card Services with IDBI bank has launched "Shagun" gift cards to contribute towards Digital India on Rupay Platform with 3 years validity and flexible loading options of amount 500 to 10000.


Banking: PMC(Punjab Maharashtra Co-operative Bank)
Due to the crisis in PMC Bank, restrictions were imposed on depositors. Now, the PMC bank generated an Expression of interest, i.e. who wants to merge PMC bank in it. Now, Centrum and BharatPe have given approval by RBI to merge PMC, but it has to make Small Finance Bank and then work for 6 months after that they will merge PMC in it. The hold of BharatPe will be 50% in newly SFB. The minimum capital requirement for SFB is 200 crores, but in this SFB they decided to contribute 900 crores, if further needed they will contribute another 900 crores.


New Launch: Wallet Auto Top-up Feature
PhonePe has launched a new Wallet Auto Top-up feature using UPI e-mandates for its customers.


New Launch: Application "Khedo Punjab"
Punjab government launched a special mobile app "Khedo Punjab" for putting sports departments and all the sportspersons of Punjab on digital platforms.


Announcement: Naomi Osaka
Japan's Naomi Osaka will not play in Wimbledon Open.


New Launch: Softex Solution
Standard Chartered Bank has launched "Softex Solution" aimed at supporting clients in their software export processes that run through the bank's digital platform Straight2Bank.

20 AND 21 June 2021


Appointment: Karim Khan
British Lawyer, Karim khan has been appointed as the chief prosecutor in International Criminal Court, The Hague, Netherland.


Appointment: Montek Singh Ahluwalia
World Bank and International Monetary Fund(IMF) formed the High-Level Advisory Group(HLAG) and appointed Montek Singh Ahluwalia as a member of it. The aim is to face the dual crises caused by Covid-19 and Climate change.


Award: Sumitra Mitra
Indian-American chemist Sumitra Mitra has been honored with the European Inventor Award 2021 in the category of "Non-European patent countries". This award is given by European Patent Office(EPO).


Announcement: Japan
Japan will start issuing vaccination certificates as Vaccine Passports.


Report: RBI
RBI is in second place for transferring reserves to the government as a percentage of GDP for the fiscal year 2020-21. While Turkey is in the first place. RBI transferred 99177 Crores to the government this year which is 73.5% more than the previous year. Previous year India was in fourth place and Singapore topped the list.


Report: Swiss Bank Money
India is at 51st place ahead of countries like New Zealand, Norway, Sweden, Denmark Hungary, Mauritius, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka in terms of foreign client money in the Swiss Bank. India's money is around (CHF 2.55 billion ), while UK is at the top(CHF 377Billion)and the USA at second place(CHF 152 Billion).


Announcement: Divyangta Khel Kendra
The government announced the establishment of five Divyangta Khel Kendras in different parts of the country. Ahmadabad is one of the cities among them.


Victory: 2021 French Grand Prix Title
Netherland's Red Bull Driver Max Verstappen has won the 2021 French Grand Prix Title.


New Campaign: Jaan Hai To Jahaan Hai
Union Minister for Minority Affairs Mukhtar Abbas Naqvi has said that a nationwide "Jaan Hai To Jahan Hai" awareness campaign will be launched to create awareness on corona vaccination in rural and remote areas of the country.


Award: Shyam Sundar Jayani
Rajasthan-based climate activist Shyam Sundar Jayani's environment conservation concept, "Familial Forestry" has won this year's prestigious United Nations' Land for Life Award. The announcement was made by UN Convention on Combat Desertification(UNCCD). This year's theme was "Healthy Land, Healthy Lives" Jayani is an associate professor in Sociology in Bikaner and has planted more than 2.5 million trees in a time period of 15 years under the "Familial Forestry" campaign.


Book: "The Nutmeg's Curse: Parables for a planet in crisis"
The book "The Nutmeg's Curse: Parables for a planet in crisis" authored by Amitav Ghosh.


Book of Poems: "Beyond here and other poems"
CM of Odhisa Naveen Patnaik has released a book of poems "Beyond here and other poems" written by senior bureaucrat Bishnupada Sethi.


Day: World Refugee Day
World Refugee Day is celebrated on 20th June. The theme of this is "Together we heal, learn and shine". Turkey is a top hosting 3.6 million refugees and Columbia at second place(1.8 Million) which includes people who fled from Venezuela in the last few years, according to a report by United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees(UNHCR) report on forced displacement.


New Scheme: Aashirbad
Odisha CM Naveen Patnaik announced a special scheme "Aashirbad" to provide financial support to the orphans.


New Scheme: Bihar
Bihar's CM Nitish Kumar launched Mukhyamantri Mahila Udymi Yojana and Mukhyamantri Yuva Udyami Yojana.


Meet: G20 Educations Ministers
Sanjay Dhotre, Minister of State for Education and Electronics and IT will represent India in the G20 Education Ministers Meet on 22nd June 2021. He will also attend the joint meeting of G20 Ministers for Education and Labour and Employment. The two meetings were going to held under the chairmanship of Italy.


Report: Sustainable Development Report 2021
India has been ranked 120 in Sustainable Development Report 2021, earlier it was at 117th rank. The top three countries are Finland, Sweden, and Denmark.


Announcement: SCO Meet
National Security Advisor Ajit Doval is expected to attend a meeting of the Shanghai Cooperation Organization in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. The SCO was formed in 2001 and includes 8 countries(India, China, Russia, Pakistan, Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, Tajikistan, Kyrgyzstan). It is headquartered in China's Beijing.


Obituary: Guruprasad Mohapatra
Secretary in the Department of Industry and Internal Trade(DPIIT) Guruprasad Mohapatra has passed away at the age of 59.

22 June 2021


First: Laurel Hubbard
New Zealand Weightlifter Laurel Hubbard will become the first transgender athlete to compete at Tokyo Olympics. Earlier, Laurel participated as Men in 2013.


Sports: Tokyo Olympics
India named striker Rani Rampal as Skipper, Deep Grace Ekka, and Savita as two vice-captains of the National Women's Hockey Team in the Tokyo Olympics.


Army: Light Utility Helicopter
The Army will receive the first batch of six indigenous Light Utility Helicopters (LUH) by the end of 2022. It was designed and developed by Hindustan Aeronautics Limited(HAL).


New Appointment: Ajay Kumar Suri
Lt. General Ajay Kumar Suri appointed as the Director-General(DG) and Colonel Commandant of the Army Aviation.


Announcement: World Athletics Championships
World Athletics Championships increased its price for two upcoming world championships, and the prize would increase by Rupees 1 million. The 2022 World Athletics Championships are going to held in Eugene, Oregon, United States, and the 2023 World Athletics Championships are in Budapest, Hungary.


Award: Actress Tillotama Shome
Indian Actress Tillotama Shome has won the Best Actor Award at the 2021 UK Asian Film Festival(UKAFF), for her role in the film "Raahgir: The Wayfarers", and it was directed by Goutam Ghose.


New Launch: Application mYoga
Prime Minister Narendra Modi launched the mYoga fitness app on the occasion of International Yoga Day on 21st June. It was jointly developed by the Ministry of Ayush and the World Health Organization to bring assisted yoga training to everyone with a smartphone and without any sign-in.

Bharti Airtel and Tata Group have announced a strategic partnership for implementing a 5G network solution for India, which will be available for commercial development from January 2022.


Appointment: Upasana Kamineni
World Wildlife Fund for Nature(WWF) has inducted Upasana Kamineni as the "Ambassador of Forest Frontline Heroes" with the aim to appreciate the efforts of frontline workers in hospitals and in the wildlife protection space. She is the Director at Apollo Hospitals.


Appointment: Pratima Murthy
Senior Psychiatrist Pratima Murthy has been appointed as the director of the National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences(NIMHANS) for 5 years.


First: Dr. Tadang Minu
Dr. Tadang Menu from Arunachal Pradesh is the First woman in the country and the Second Indian who has been appointed to the Coaches Committee of the International Boxing Association(AIBA).


Appointment: Teli Khali
Boxing Federation of India(BFI) has appointed Teli Khali from Arunachal Pradesh as its Youth Commission Secretary.

National Human Rights Commission(NHRC) has constituted a committee to enquire into the complaints of post-poll violence in West Bengal. It will be headed by NHRC Member, Rajeev Jain.


Book: The 7 Sins of Being A Mother
Film-maker-writer Tahira Kashyap Khurrana has announced her upcoming book about motherhood titled, "The 7 Sins of Being A Mother".


Announcement: Savings of housewives
A housewife now June not face any problem from the Income Tax Department on the deposit of cash up to Rupees 2.5 lacs during demonetization.


Report: UN Conference for Trade and Development(UNCTAD)
India received USD 64 billion in Foreign Direct Investment in 2020, the fifth-largest recipient of inflows in the world according to a UN report. It was increased to 27% in comparison to 2019, where FDI was 51 billion USD. Global FDI flows were affected by pandemics and contracted by 35% in comparison to 2019(1.5 trillion USD) and 2020(1 trillion USD). India ranked 18 out of the world's top 20 economies FDI outflows, India's FDI outflow was 12 billion USD while in 2019 it was 13 billion USD.


New Facility: ICICI Bank
ICICI Bank has introduced an instant cardless EMI facility for online purchases on e-commerce platforms.


New Appointment: Vijay Deshwal
Vijay Deshwal has joined as a group CEO of Magma Fincorp, which was recently acquired by the Poonawala group.


New Programme: REVV Up
As part of the year of AI 2020 initiative, the Telangana government launched the Telangana Artificial Intelligence Mission accelerator program REVV Up, which is exclusively designed for growth-stage artificial intelligence(AI) startups.


New Council: Economic Advisory Council
Tamil Nadu constituted a five-member economic advisory council to guclasse CM MK Stalin in managing the state's economic and social policies. The council includes Esther Duflo(Nobel Prize-Winning Economist), Raghuram Rajan(Former RBI Governor), Arvind Subramanian(EX Chief Economic Advisor), Jean Dreze(Welfare economist and social scientist), S Narayan(Former Union Finance Secretary).
